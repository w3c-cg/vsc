Abstract

This specification defines the Universal Object & Resource Attestation (UORA) protocol, a vendor-neutral, decentralized framework for the identity, state verification, and lifecycle tracking of physical assets. UORA bridges the gap between physical objects and digital identifiers by enabling every resource to function as a first-class citizen of the internet. The protocol leverages W3C's Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs) and Verifiable Credentials (VCs) to create a unified, interoperable system for asset provenance, custody tracking, and lifecycle management across global supply chains.

1. Introduction

Physical assets—from pharmaceutical products to automotive parts, from luxury goods to critical minerals— form the backbone of the global economy. Yet these assets remain largely invisible to the decentralized web. Their identities are managed through centralized systems, their histories fragmented across siloed databases, and their provenance difficult to verify.

The Universal Object & Resource Attestation (UORA) protocol addresses this gap by providing a standardized, decentralized framework for assigning persistent identifiers to physical objects and tracking their lifecycle through cryptographically verifiable attestations.

1.1 Problem Statement

Current systems for tracking physical assets suffer from several fundamental limitations:

  • Centralized Control: Asset identifiers are controlled by centralized registries that can be revoked or manipulated.
  • Fragmented Provenance: Supply chain data is scattered across incompatible systems, making end-to-end traceability difficult.
  • Verifiability Gaps: Claims about asset origin, custody, and condition lack cryptographic proof.
  • Interoperability Barriers: No universal standard exists for asset identification and attestation across industries.
  • Physical-Digital Gap: The link between physical objects and their digital representations is often weak or easily counterfeited.

1.2 Solution Overview

UORA addresses these challenges through four core components:

  1. Universal Addressing: Each physical asset receives a globally unique, decentralized identifier (DID) that is self-sovereign and cryptographically verifiable.
  2. Event-Based Attestations: The asset's lifecycle is documented through Verifiable Credentials that attest to key events: origin, transfer, transformation, and disposition.
  3. Secure Physical Binding: Standardized methods for linking digital DIDs to physical anchors (NFC, QR, cryptographic hardware, IoT sensors) ensure tamper-evident possession verification.
  4. Interoperability Framework: Clear guidelines for integrating UORA with existing business systems and legacy standards enable practical adoption.

1.3 Conformance

2. Terminology

Asset
A physical object that can be uniquely identified and tracked through its lifecycle.
Attestation
A verifiable claim about an asset's state, event, or characteristic, expressed as a Verifiable Credential.
Asset DID
A Decentralized Identifier assigned to a physical asset, enabling it to be uniquely and persistently identified.
Event-Based Attestation
A Verifiable Credential that documents a specific event in an asset's lifecycle (origin, transfer, transformation, disposition).
Physical Anchor
A physical element (NFC tag, QR code, cryptographic chip, IoT sensor) that binds an Asset DID to the physical object.
Holder
The entity currently in possession of an asset and its associated DID.
Issuer
An entity authorized to create attestations about an asset (e.g., manufacturer, logistics provider, auditor).
Verifier
An entity that validates attestations to verify asset provenance, custody, or condition.

3. UORA Architecture

The UORA protocol defines a layered architecture that separates concerns and enables modular implementation.

3.1 Addressing Layer

Every physical asset tracked under UORA MUST be assigned a unique Decentralized Identifier (DID). Asset DIDs SHOULD be generated using the did:key method or another method that ensures self-sovereign control.

Example: Asset DID Generation

{
  "did": "did:key:z6Mk2c9LiqwxsnFYhPj7TNwe-Cj_R8yVRw-ISCH40lqfcbs",
  "publicKey": "z6Mk2c9LiqwxsnFYhPj7TNwe-Cj_R8yVRw-ISCH40lqfcbs",
  "assetType": "batch",
  "batchNumber": "LOT-2025-001",
  "productCode": "NDC-12345-678-90"
}

3.1.1 Asset Granularity Levels

Assets MAY be identified at three levels of granularity:

Level Description Use Case
Batch Group of identical items produced together Pharmaceuticals, food products, chemicals
SKU Stock Keeping Unit (product line) Consumer goods, electronics, apparel
Serial Individual, uniquely identifiable item Luxury goods, medical devices, automotive parts

3.1.2 Asset DID Document

The DID Document for an asset SHOULD include additional metadata about the asset, using the alsoKnownAs property to reference existing identifiers (e.g., GS1 GTIN, serial numbers). 

{
  "@context": "https://www.w3.org/ns/did/v1",
  "id": "did:key:z6Mk2c9LiqwxsnFYhPj7TNwe-Cj_R8yVRw-ISCH40lqfcbs",
  "alsoKnownAs": [
    "https://example.com/batch/LOT-2025-001",
    "urn:epc:id:sgtin:0614141.123456.001"
  ],
  "verificationMethod": [{
    "id": "did:key:z6Mk2c9LiqwxsnFYhPj7TNwe-Cj_R8yVRw-ISCH40lqfcbs#keys-1",
    "type": "Ed25519VerificationKey2020",
    "controller": "did:key:z6Mk2c9LiqwxsnFYhPj7TNwe-Cj_R8yVRw-ISCH40lqfcbs",
    "publicKeyMultibase": "z6Mk2c9LiqwxsnFYhPj7TNwe-Cj_R8yVRw-ISCH40lqfcbs"
  }],
  "authentication": [
    "did:key:z6Mk2c9LiqwxsnFYhPj7TNwe-Cj_R8yVRw-ISCH40lqfcbs#keys-1"
  ],
  "service": [{
    "id": "did:key:z6Mk2c9LiqwxsnFYhPj7TNwe-Cj_R8yVRw-ISCH40lqfcbs#asset",
    "type": "UORAAsset",
    "serviceEndpoint": "https://example.com/assets/LOT-2025-001",
    "assetMetadata": {
      "productName": "Example Pharmaceutical",
      "batchNumber": "LOT-2025-001",
      "manufacturingDate": "2025-03-31T00:00:00Z",
      "expirationDate": "2026-03-30T23:59:59Z"
    }
  }]
}

3.2 Attestation Layer

Asset lifecycle events are documented using Verifiable Credentials. UORA defines four core event types, which SHALL form the foundation of asset provenance.

3.2.1 Origin Attestation

An Origin Attestation documents the creation, manufacturing, or extraction of an asset. It SHOULD include information about the producer, location, time, and relevant certifications. 

{
  "@context": [
    "https://www.w3.org/ns/credentials/v2",
    "https://w3id.org/uora/v1"
  ],
  "type": ["VerifiableCredential", "UORAOrigin"],
  "issuer": "did:web:manufacturer.example.com",
  "credentialSubject": {
    "id": "did:key:z6Mk2c9LiqwxsnFYhPj7TNwe-Cj_R8yVRw-ISCH40lqfcbs",
    "origin": {
      "type": "Manufacturing",
      "producer": {
        "id": "did:web:manufacturer.example.com",
        "name": "Example Pharmaceuticals"
      },
      "location": {
        "address": "123 Pharma Lane, City, State, ZIP",
        "coordinates": "40.7128° N, 74.0060° W",
        "country": "US"
      },
      "timestamp": "2025-03-31T10:00:00Z",
      "batchNumber": "LOT-2025-001",
      "manufacturingDate": "2025-03-31T00:00:00Z",
      "expirationDate": "2026-03-30T23:59:59Z",
      "certifications": [
        {
          "type": "GMP",
          "issuer": "FDA",
          "reference": "GMP-2025-12345"
        }
      ]
    }
  },
  "proof": {
    "type": "Ed25519Signature2020",
    "created": "2025-03-31T10:00:00Z",
    "verificationMethod": "did:web:manufacturer.example.com#keys-1",
    "proofPurpose": "assertionMethod",
    "proofValue": "z58DzUq..."
  }
}

3.2.2 Transfer Attestation

A Transfer Attestation documents a change of custody or ownership. It SHALL include the sender, recipient, location, and timestamp of the transfer. 

{
  "@context": [
    "https://www.w3.org/ns/credentials/v2",
    "https://w3id.org/uora/v1"
  ],
  "type": ["VerifiableCredential", "UORATransfer"],
  "issuer": "did:web:logistics.example.com",
  "credentialSubject": {
    "id": "did:key:z6Mk2c9LiqwxsnFYhPj7TNwe-Cj_R8yVRw-ISCH40lqfcbs",
    "transfer": {
      "from": {
        "holder": "did:web:manufacturer.example.com",
        "timestamp": "2025-03-31T10:00:00Z",
        "location": {
          "address": "123 Pharma Lane, City, State, ZIP"
        }
      },
      "to": {
        "holder": "did:web:distributor.example.com",
        "timestamp": "2025-04-01T14:30:00Z",
        "location": {
          "address": "456 Distribution Ave, City, State, ZIP"
        }
      },
      "carrier": {
        "id": "did:web:carrier.example.com",
        "name": "Global Logistics",
        "trackingNumber": "1Z999AA10123456784"
      }
    }
  },
  "proof": { ... }
}

3.2.3 Transformation Attestation

A Transformation Attestation documents processing, assembly, or modification of an asset. It SHALL include input assets, output assets, and the transformation process. 

{
  "@context": [
    "https://www.w3.org/ns/credentials/v2",
    "https://w3id.org/uora/v1"
  ],
  "type": ["VerifiableCredential", "UORATransformation"],
  "issuer": "did:web:processor.example.com",
  "credentialSubject": {
    "inputs": [
      "did:key:z6Mk2c9LiqwxsnFYhPj7TNwe-Cj_R8yVRw-ISCH40lqfcbs",
      "did:key:z6Mk2c9LiqwxsnFYhPj7TNwe-Cj_R8yVRw-ISCH40lqfcbd"
    ],
    "outputs": [
      "did:key:z6Mk2c9LiqwxsnFYhPj7TNwe-Cj_R8yVRw-ISCH40lqfcbe"
    ],
    "transformation": {
      "type": "Assembly",
      "process": "A-2025-001",
      "timestamp": "2025-04-15T09:00:00Z",
      "location": {
        "address": "789 Assembly Way, City, State, ZIP"
      },
      "qualityChecks": [
        {
          "check": "Visual Inspection",
          "result": "Pass",
          "inspector": "did:web:inspector.example.com#QA-001"
        }
      ]
    }
  },
  "proof": { ... }
}

3.2.4 Disposition Attestation

A Disposition Attestation documents the end-of-life of an asset, including recycling, decommissioning, or destruction. It SHALL include the method, location, and responsible party. 

{
  "@context": [
    "https://www.w3.org/ns/credentials/v2",
    "https://w3id.org/uora/v1"
  ],
  "type": ["VerifiableCredential", "UORADisposition"],
  "issuer": "did:web:recycler.example.com",
  "credentialSubject": {
    "id": "did:key:z6Mk2c9LiqwxsnFYhPj7TNwe-Cj_R8yVRw-ISCH40lqfcbs",
    "disposition": {
      "type": "Recycling",
      "timestamp": "2025-12-31T10:00:00Z",
      "location": {
        "address": "321 Recycling Center, City, State, ZIP"
      },
      "responsibleParty": {
        "id": "did:web:recycler.example.com",
        "name": "Green Recycling Inc."
      },
      "certificate": {
        "number": "RC-2025-12345",
        "standard": "ISO 14001"
      }
    }
  },
  "proof": { ... }
}

3.3 Secure Physical Binding

The link between an Asset DID and the physical object MUST be established through a tamper-evident physical anchor. UORA supports multiple binding methods.

3.3.1 Binding Methods

Method Description Security Level
NFC/RFID Cryptographic tag containing the Asset DID or DID URL High (with secure element)
QR Code Printed QR encoding the Asset DID URL Medium (copiable but traceable)
Cryptographic Hardware Secure element with private key for authentication Very High
IoT Sensors Embedded sensors with DID-enabled communication High

3.3.2 Proof of Binding

To verify possession and authenticity, the holder SHALL be able to demonstrate control over both the physical anchor and the associated DID.

Example: Proof of Binding using NFC challenge-response

{
  "challenge": "n once random challenge",
  "response": "signed challenge using DID's private key",
  "anchorType": "NFC",
  "anchorId": "04:5F:3A:2B:1C:9D",
  "timestamp": "2025-03-31T10:00:00Z"
}

4. Asset DID Resolution

Asset DIDs SHALL be resolvable to DID Documents containing asset metadata and service endpoints. Resolution MAY be performed through:

  • Universal Resolver - W3C Universal DID Resolver
  • DID Method-Specific Resolvers - Native to the DID method used
  • Cached Resolution - For performance in high-throughput environments

Implementers SHOULD cache resolved DID Documents to reduce latency, but MUST respect cache TTL values and honor revocation mechanisms.

5. Interoperability with Legacy Systems

UORA SHALL provide clear mappings to existing supply chain standards to enable practical adoption.

5.1 GS1 EPCIS Mapping

GS1 EPCIS events MAY be mapped to UORA attestations as follows:

EPCIS Event Type UORA Attestation Mapping Notes
ObjectEvent (commissioning) Origin Attestation First recorded event becomes origin
ObjectEvent (shipping/receiving) Transfer Attestation BizStep determines sender/recipient
AggregationEvent Transformation Attestation Child EPCS become inputs
DispositionEvent Disposition Attestation End-of-life events

5.2 ISO Standards Alignment

UORA aligns with the following ISO standards:

  • ISO 28000 - Supply Chain Security: UORA attestations provide verifiable chain of custody
  • ISO 9001 - Quality Management: Attestations support quality traceability
  • ISO 14001 - Environmental Management: Disposition attestations document recycling
  • ISO 27001 - Information Security: Cryptographic verification ensures integrity

6. Security Considerations

6.1 Key Management and Recovery

Asset DIDs MUST be managed with appropriate key recovery mechanisms. The [[DID-KR]] specification provides a framework for social recovery that SHOULD be implemented for high-value assets.

6.2 Tamper Evidence

Physical anchors SHALL be designed to provide tamper evidence. RECOMMENDED approaches include:

  • Cryptographic seals that break upon removal
  • Secure elements with anti-tamper circuitry
  • Blockchain-anchored audit trails
  • Time-stamped attestations of integrity checks

6.3 Replay Attack Prevention

All proof-of-binding mechanisms SHALL incorporate nonces or timestamps to prevent replay attacks. Verifiers MUST validate freshness of proofs.

7. Privacy Considerations

7.1 Selective Disclosure

UORA attestations SHOULD leverage the selective disclosure capabilities of Verifiable Credentials, enabling holders to reveal only necessary information to verifiers.

7.2 Data Minimization

Asset DID Documents SHOULD NOT contain sensitive information unless absolutely necessary. Such information SHOULD be stored off-ledger and referenced via service endpoints with appropriate access controls.

B. Use Case Examples

B.1 Pharmaceutical Supply Chain

A pharmaceutical manufacturer uses UORA to track a batch of medication from production to patient. Each batch receives a DID at commissioning. Origin attestation includes GMP certification. Transfer attestations document each custody change from manufacturer to distributor to pharmacy. The final dispensation to a patient is recorded as a final transfer, enabling recall capabilities.

B.2 Critical Minerals Traceability

A mining company uses UORA to document conflict-free tantalum from mine to smelter to electronics manufacturer. Origin attestations include mine location, extraction date, and ethical sourcing certifications. Transformation attestations document smelting and refining processes. The final electronic component carries the full provenance chain, verifiable at point of sale.

B.3 Luxury Goods Authentication

A luxury watch manufacturer embeds an NFC chip with the watch's DID. The chip is cryptographically bound to the physical watch. At each service, authorized watchmakers issue maintenance attestations. Buyers can verify authenticity and service history by scanning the watch with their phone, receiving verifiable proofs of origin and service.
